Kinematics (운동학) Summary Notes

Posted on 2025.08.30

Definition

Kinematics: The study of motion itself, without considering the forces that cause it.
운동학: 힘의 원인을 고려하지 않고 물체의 움직임 자체를 분석하는 학문.

Vectors & Scalars

  • Vector (벡터): Defined by both magnitude and direction. Requires geometric or component-based operations.
  • Scalar (스칼라): Defined only by magnitude. Follows ordinary arithmetic rules.

Basic Quantities

  • Position (위치): Location relative to a reference point.
  • Distance (거리): Total path length traveled.
  • Displacement (변위): Straight-line distance from start to end point (vector quantity).
  • Speed (속력): Distance traveled per unit time (scalar).
  • Velocity (속도): Displacement per unit time including direction (vector).
  • Acceleration (가속도): Rate of change of velocity. Zero acceleration means uniform motion.

Kinematic Equations (등가속도 운동 방정식)

For motion under constant acceleration a:

  • \( v = v_0 + at \)
  • \( \Delta x = v_0 t + \tfrac{1}{2} a t^2 \)
  • \( v^2 = v_0^2 + 2a \Delta x \)
  • \( \Delta x = \tfrac{1}{2}(v_0 + v) t \)

Vector Addition (합성 벡터)

Two or more vectors can be combined into a single resultant vector.

Example: A 5 N force acts east, and a 12 N force acts south. The resultant vector is:

Magnitude: \( R = \sqrt{5^2 + 12^2} = 13\,N \)

Direction: \( \theta = \arctan(12/5) \approx 67^\circ \) south of east.
